Indian Laws That Protect You

Your real legal protections as a patient in India. Use these in every negotiation.

Clinical Establishments Act, 2010

Requires all registered hospitals to display service rates, provide itemised bills on request, and follow standard treatment protocols. Hospitals cannot refuse an itemised bill.

Consumer Protection Act, 2019

Medical overcharging is a "deficiency in service." You can file a complaint at District, State, or National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions (NCDRC) with no fee for claims under ₹5 lakh.

Ayushman Bharat / PM-JAY

Empanelled hospitals must charge pre-fixed package rates for covered procedures. Overcharging PM-JAY beneficiaries is illegal and can result in de-empanelment of the hospital.

CGHS Rate Card (MoHFW)

The Ministry of Health publishes standardised CGHS rates for 1,600+ procedures. Courts and consumer forums use these as the national fair-price benchmark in disputes against private hospitals.

NMC Code of Ethics

The National Medical Commission forbids doctors from charging excessive fees, ordering unnecessary tests for financial gain, or engaging in "cut practice" with labs and hospitals.

IRDAI / Bima Bharosa

IRDAI mandates cashless claim settlement within 1 hour of discharge. If a hospital inflates bills for insured patients or delays discharge, file a complaint at IRDAI's Bima Bharosa portal.
